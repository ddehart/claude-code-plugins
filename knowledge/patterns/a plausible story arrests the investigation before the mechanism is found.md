# a plausible story arrests the investigation before the mechanism is found

A symptom matches an available explanation well enough that diagnosis stops there. The stopping is
invisible, because a fitting story feels like an answer. Opening the artifact overturns it — and the
overturned version was confident and specific, not tentative.

Four instances, all self-caught, all corrected only by reading the thing rather than reasoning about it: a
diagnosis of where generated guidance was lost, overturned on checking; a reported coherence conflict later
conceded as overstated; two "agent discrepancies" that were the verifier's own measurement errors; a ledger
read where substring presence matched the expected story so cleanly the actual test went unchecked.

A satisfying *number* ends a search as effectively as a satisfying story — "four out of four" reads as
having ruled something out, when four outcomes under one shared brief cannot separate the brief from the
mechanism.

The counter-practice is visible in the same record: several times a matching story was refused and the
artifact opened instead. So the pattern is not that the check never fires — it is that **whether it fires
is unforced**, and fluency at having just described the failure does not force it.

*One witness (2026-07-22). Open.*
