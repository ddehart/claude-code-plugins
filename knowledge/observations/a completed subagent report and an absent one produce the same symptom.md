Four inspection readers each completed a full transcript read, composed structured findings, and went idle
without the orchestrator receiving anything — because the brief told them to report "in your final
message," which for a mailbox teammate is delivered nowhere. Two later confirmed independently that
complete write-ups existed and had been sent twice into that channel. Completion and delivery are separate
failure modes with one indistinguishable symptom: a briefing that warns about the symptom addresses only
the motivational cause and cannot touch the structural one. The reliable fix is an exit that requires the
reporting call, not a stronger warning.
