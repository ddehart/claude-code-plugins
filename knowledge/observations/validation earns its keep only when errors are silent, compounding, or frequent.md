The decision rule that collapsed the validation layer, applied check-by-check down a 42-item inventory:
validation earns its keep only when errors are silent, compounding, or frequent. The errors it guarded
against were none of the three — a broken wikilink self-reveals (Obsidian renders it visibly), doesn't
compound, and is a ten-second fix on notice. Machinery that guards self-revealing, cheap, rare errors is
paying a standing cost against a problem that corrects itself.
