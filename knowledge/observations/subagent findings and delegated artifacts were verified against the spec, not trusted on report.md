Authoring was fanned out to cheaper models and then each artifact was read back against the spec — the
audits caught real defects in four of five (a stamp-format contradiction, a missing source-note step,
dangling spec references, a missing map). Agent factual claims were also checked directly rather than
trusted: two of four finders falsely claimed advice "violated a global rule," caught by timezone forensics
showing the rule didn't exist yet; a process-assessor initially checked the wrong commons repo and nearly
reported a promotion as a no-op. Trust nothing load-bearing on an agent's report alone.
