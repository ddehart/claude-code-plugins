# the commons is the evidence tier beneath behavioral rules

The cross-domain commons is not a rival to `~/.claude/rules/` but the epistemic tier underneath them:
rules are conclusions with their evidence buried in git history, and the commons is where that evidence
lives as atomic claims. Knowledge in the commons is consulted on demand ("what do we know about X"), never
ambiently injected into every session — the value of ambient recall is too rare to pay for on each turn. A
claim graduates into a steering rule deliberately, with human approval, and only once its evidence spans
more than one domain.
